Mexico City vs New York cost of living

New York is one of the most expensive cities in the US. Mexico City can be significantly cheaper, but your real budget depends on lifestyle and neighborhood choices.

Quick comparison

Housing

NYC rent is typically the biggest cost driver. In Mexico City, rent can vary a lot depending on areas like Roma, Condesa, Polanco, or more budget-friendly zones.

Food

Eating local in Mexico City can be cheaper than NYC. Imported groceries and premium restaurants can narrow the gap.

Transportation

Mexico City has affordable public transport. NYC also has public transport, but total costs vary depending on commute and habits.

Healthcare

Private healthcare in Mexico can be less expensive than the US, but coverage and preferences matter. Always budget for insurance and emergencies.

Typical lifestyle scenarios

Local lifestyle

Local groceries, public transport, modest rent outside premium zones. Often the lowest cost option.

Expats and premium areas

Neighborhoods like Roma, Condesa, Polanco, imported groceries, frequent dining out. Costs can rise quickly.

What to budget for when moving

  • Upfront housing costs: deposit, agency fees (if any), furniture
  • Health insurance or emergency fund
  • Mobile plan and home internet
  • Transport: metro, ride-hailing, occasional flights
  • Imported items if you rely on US brands
